\section{Orchestration and Dataflow}
\label{sec:orchestration}

\begin{itemize}
    \item This section examines how radar data is moved, buffered, and scheduled through the pipeline, and how these choices affect latency and throughput.

    \item \textbf{Threads and Batching:} We will compare threading and batching strategies to understand the trade-off between throughput and end-to-end delay.

    \item \textbf{Reading data:} As a baseline, input will be read from \texttt{stdin} streams, then compared against lower-overhead approaches such as direct memory access into kernel buffers. The \texttt{vivid} virtual video device will be used for controlled testing.

    \item \textbf{Writing data:} We will compare whether stages should communicate through standard application pipes or through lower-copy shared-memory style interfaces.

    \item \textbf{Aggregating Data Streams:} Data from the four AWR chips must be combined with minimal delay while remaining robust to missing or late packets. We will compare strict synchronisation against timeout-based and best-effort aggregation policies.
\end{itemize}
